General Information about Neuss

Neuss is a city in the German state of North Rhine-Westphalia. It is located on the west bank of the Rhine, opposite Düsseldorf. Founded by the Romans in 16 BC, it is one of the oldest cities in Germany.

The city is known for its Roman past, its impressive Quirinus-Münster, and its annual Schützenfest (Riflemen's Fair), one of the largest of its kind in the world.

Neuss is also an important industrial and commercial center, with a significant port on the Rhine. It offers a high quality of life with green spaces, cultural institutions, and good transport connections.

Population: Approximately 150,000 (as of recent estimates)

Area: Approximately 99.5 km²

Dialing code: +49 (Germany) 2131 (Neuss)
